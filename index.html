History of the font

Bild is a typeface created by David Jonathan Ross for his font of the month series. This font doesn’t tell designers much about how it wants to be used because it isn’t incredibly generic or specific, but it does “set a damn fine headline.” The first publications of Bild focused on the font’s width, with only a black weight available, but what set it apart from other condensed sans serif fonts was its mixture of structured and organic forms. These few inconsistencies prevent Bild from becoming super mechanical: curvy shapes alongside rigid ones, non-horizontal inner-terminals on letters such as C, a, G, S, and asymmetrical arches on h, m, and n’s. The appeal of this font left the people (and Ross himself) wanting more. In 2020, he added weights for the compressed width and released it as font of the month in July 2023 with a full set of weights. But it still wasn’t complete. In August 2023, Bild was font of the month once again, this time with the addition of a variable x-height axis and two more compressed widths. Now completed (we think), Bild is a very strong typeface that can be used in many settings. Ross also noted that Bild means “image” in German.
